## Short homepage introduction

Cells do not change identity all at once. They pass through intermediate states, accumulate regulatory changes, and diverge into distinct subpopulations. My work focuses on this transitional space, using single-cell transcriptomics, trajectory inference, gene regulatory network reconstruction, CNV-aware analysis, and multi-omics reasoning to turn complex biological data into mechanistically meaningful hypotheses.

## About section

I am a computational biology and bioinformatics researcher with a background in Computer Science and Mathematics. My research focuses on cell-state transitions in complex biological systems, especially how cellular identities shift during disease progression.

I use trajectory inference, gene regulatory network reconstruction, CNV-aware interpretation, and multi-omics integration not as endpoints, but as tools to ask a deeper question: what regulatory logic drives a cell to abandon one state and commit to another?

My strongest current work is in cancer single-cell genomics. In a human thyroid cancer project, I lead computational analyses of single-cell transcriptomic data to study the transition from normal thyroid epithelial states to papillary thyroid carcinoma and anaplastic thyroid carcinoma. I build reproducible workflows that connect high-dimensional data analysis with biological interpretation and experimentally testable hypotheses.

Beyond cancer, I am interested in computational biology problems involving single-cell and spatial omics, systems biology, regulatory genomics, biomedical machine learning, perturbation modeling, and high-dimensional biological data analysis.

I am looking for PhD opportunities where rigorous computational methods can help answer ambitious biological questions.

## Design direction

The website should feel:
modern, scientific, elegant, serious, clear, research-focused, and suitable for PhD applications in computational biology.

The homepage should not feel limited only to cancer. Cancer should appear as Fatemeh’s strongest current application area, while the broader identity should be computational biology, cell-state transitions, regulatory mechanisms, and high-dimensional biomedical data.

Avoid:
childish animations, random floating icons, generic developer-portfolio design, excessive gradients, fake 3D, low-contrast text, and too many moving elements.

## Interaction direction

Interaction should help explain scientific work.

Useful interactions:

* project filtering by method/topic
* scroll-based research timeline
* hoverable research map
* project pipeline diagrams
* subtle card hover effects

Avoid:

* bouncing cards
* spinning icons
* unnecessary 3D
* animations that distract from the research
